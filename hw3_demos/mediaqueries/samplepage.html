Overview

The Australian Silky Terrier is a small dog breed known for its long, fine, and silky coat. They are active, alert, and often described as having a big personality in a small body. Typically, they weigh between 8 to 10 pounds and stand around 10 to 11 inches tall at the shoulder.

Care and Maintenance

Silky Terriers require regular grooming to keep their coats looking their best. Brushing should be done every other day to avoid tangles and mats. Their ears and eyes should be cleaned regularly, and their teeth should be brushed often.

History

The Australian Silky Terrier originated in Australia in the late 19th century. The breed was developed by crossing the Australian Terrier with the Yorkshire Terrier, aiming to produce a small yet strong and agile terrier. They were initially bred for hunting small game.

Health

Silky Terriers are generally healthy dogs, but they can be prone to certain health conditions, including patellar luxation, dental issues, and heart problems. Regular check-ups and a balanced diet can help maintain their well-being.

Training

Silky Terriers are intelligent and quick learners, but they can also be independent and stubborn. Early socialization and positive reinforcement training are key to a well-behaved dog. They respond well to short, consistent training sessions.
